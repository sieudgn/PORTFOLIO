Pop pop boat

Have you ever seen the movie “Ponyo on the Cliff”? A long time ago, the movie made me curious about the boat of Sosuke that was operated in an abnormal way. I wondered how the boat could move forward just by the heat of a candle. Also, his boat does not have a motor, or even a propeller. After a few years, I saw that kind of boats again in a video of The Action Lab and found out that boat was called “pop pop boat”. You can find out how the boat works here. Mr. Steve Mould has a glass version of that boat, and he clearly explained its mechanism.

Arduino Joystick

When I was 10, I had a remote-control c ar, but I could not control the car exactly. To illustrate, I could only run it at t e maximum power and turn the wheel at the maximum angle as well. I did not feel satisfi ed about this limitation because of how it worked. Therefore, I made a version of mine u sing a servo to turn left and right and a module to adjust the speed. I also used a gyros cope sensor for the input of the servo and a joystick for the input of the motor. I hoped I can make the toy become more like a real car.

Fan with Led

I used to see an interesting p roduct from my cousin. That was a small electric fan with several LEDs on its propeller. When it was in use, those LEDs were turned on and off at the exact moment. Therefore, a small text was formed by those leaders. Later, I studied A rduino and figured out its principle. It works based on the persistence of vision (also sensors of the camera). If I used Arduino to control LEDs in a precise way, I would create a low-resolution screen with a fan and some LEDs. The video below i s my small project.
